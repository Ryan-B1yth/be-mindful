History

Yoga is a centuries old practice, combining exercise and meditation through movement, flexibility, and balance. Originating in ancient India, it is a philosophical school of Hinduism and has been shown to reduce stress and improve health â€“ both physical and mental. At its creation, yoga practices were written in Sanskrit, a sacred language from South Asia. Nowadays, whilst the names may have changed, the poses and the movements are all very similar to their original texts.

Varients

The two most common forms of yoga are Hatha and Vinyasa. Hatha focuses on deliberate movements into a pose and holding the position for several breaths. Vinyasa on the other hand is the movement from one pose to the next in a smooth flow following the breath. Both varients build strength and flexibility - but Hatha builds a strong core through holding positions for longer perods of time, whereas Vinyasa improves stamina and flow through constant movement. Practicing

The beauty of yoga is that all positions can be tailored to meet a persons capabilities. Many aspects of each pose can be modified so that anyone can participate. If you're just starting out, the minimal gear you will require is a yoga mat and possibly yoga blocks, though these are not necessary. As long as you have an place you can practice with enough space around you, you should be fine. You can find videos to yoga for beginners here.
